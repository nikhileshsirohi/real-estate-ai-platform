# Demo Listing Inventory Snapshot

## Summary
This document summarizes the current seeded property-listings inventory used by the local search and recommendation flows. It is a demo inventory snapshot, not an official market dataset.

## Key Facts
- The current seed inventory includes listings across San Francisco, San Jose, Oakland, San Diego, and Sacramento.
- Oakland demo listings currently span roughly the low `$600k` range for starter condos to about `$1.49M` for larger family houses.
- Sacramento remains the lowest-priced city in the current demo inventory, with entry-level apartments and condos below many Bay Area alternatives.
- San Francisco and San Jose listings skew higher overall, especially for family-sized units with 3 or more bedrooms.
- Condos and apartments in the seeded inventory usually represent lower entry-price options than detached houses in the same city.

## City Notes
### Oakland
- Demo Oakland inventory includes urban condos, townhouses, and larger houses.
- Listings mentioning BART or stronger commute access should be preferred for transit-oriented searches.
- Starter-level Oakland options exist in the condo segment, while larger family-oriented houses sit much higher in price.

### San Jose
- Demo San Jose inventory mixes employer-adjacent apartments with more spacious family houses.
- North San Jose and Downtown listings are better suited for entry or commuter-oriented searches than Almaden or Evergreen family houses.

### San Francisco
- Demo San Francisco inventory remains expensive even for smaller condos.
- Transit- and walkability-oriented searches are more likely to surface apartment or condo products than detached houses.

### San Diego
- Demo San Diego inventory shows a split between coastal premium pricing and more moderate urban or inland options.
- Family-oriented house searches tend to surface Clairemont or North Park style listings before coastal luxury products.

### Sacramento
- Demo Sacramento inventory offers the strongest affordability within the current seed set.
- Buyers with tighter budgets are more likely to find matching options in Midtown, Downtown, Natomas, Curtis Park, or East Sacramento than in Bay Area cities.

## Advisory Use
- Use this document when a query needs a city-level explanation of why some search results are more or less affordable in the demo inventory.
- Do not present this document as official market evidence. It is internal demo context derived from the seeded listing set.
